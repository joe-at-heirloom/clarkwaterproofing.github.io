Warning Signs

Is Your Basement Trying to Tell You Something?

Most leaks start small. If you notice any of these, your foundation is asking for help:

Damp, musty smell in the basement

Water stains or seepage lines on walls

Puddles after rain or snow melt

Cracks in foundation walls

Rust on appliances or fixtures

Mold or mildew on walls or stored items

These aren't "normal basement problems." They're signs that water pressure is building up outside your home and finding its way in.

The Right Fix

Why Exterior Waterproofing Is the Only Permanent Solution

Why Your Basement Could Be Leaking

Metro Detroit basements leak because of hydrostatic pressure — wet soil pressing against your foundation. Over time, that pressure forces water through small cracks, joints, and gaps.

  • Small foundation cracks and deteriorated mortar joints
  • Older, clogged, or collapsed drain tile
  • Gaps around basement windows or utility entries
  • Rising spring groundwater and heavy summer storms
When water is pushing on the outside of the wall, the only permanent fix is to relieve that pressure and waterproof the exterior surface.

Why Interior Waterproofing Doesn't Fix the Problem

Interior systems are designed to collect water after it comes through your wall. They hide symptoms instead of fixing the structure.

  • Water still enters your foundation wall and footing
  • Freeze–thaw cycles in winter widen cracks every year
  • Constant moisture weakens concrete over time
  • Moisture inside the wall can feed mold and mildew
Exterior waterproofing stops water before it enters the wall, protecting both your living space and your foundation. Interior waterproofing will only make the problem worse.
